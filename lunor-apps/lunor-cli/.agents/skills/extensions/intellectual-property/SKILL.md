# Intellectual Property

## Instructions

Use this skill when code, generated assets, third-party content, or user-owned designs may have ownership or licensing implications.

1. Identify all external assets, datasets, fonts, templates, and code snippets.
2. Record source, license, attribution requirements, and redistribution limits.
3. Avoid embedding proprietary content in examples, fixtures, screenshots, or logs.
4. Flag ambiguous ownership as a product/legal decision, not an engineering assumption.
5. Prefer generated placeholders or clearly licensed assets for tests and docs.

## Review Checklist

- Third-party assets have license metadata.
- Generated assets do not imitate protected brand marks.
- Customer-owned data is not persisted outside the approved boundary.
- Attribution requirements are documented.
